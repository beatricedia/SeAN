Pollen allergy

Symptoms

Allergic symptoms tend to be particularly intense on cool days when the wind rises and the pollen is transported through the air. On rainy days, on the other hand, there is a decrease in the amount of pollen in air. Allergy manifestations include:

  • rhinorrhoea, eyes tearing
  • sneezing
  • cough
  • itching of the nose and eyes
  • swelling of the eyes.

Treatment

Treatments for spring allergies include many non-prescription drugs. These drugs are effective in most cases and include:

  • antihistamines - relieves allergy symptoms by lowering histamine levels
  • decongestants - helps relieve nasal passages and relieve congestion and inflammation
  • nasal sprays with cromolyn sodium - can help prevent seasonal allergic rhinitis by limiting histamine production before it triggers allergic symptoms
  • eye drops - to relieve itching and eye lacrimation.

Even if you can purchase these medicines without a prescription, it is advisable to talk with your doctor before using them to make sure you are being treated appropriately and safely. Some antihistamines may cause the feeling of being sleepy (so you need to be careful when when you take them during the day). Do not use antihistamines and decongestants released without prescription medical, more than a few days without first talking to your doctor.

Sun allergy

Symptoms

In most cases, sun allergy suffers from severe rashes and reddened skin. Sometimes blisters, pustules, bleeding or intense pruritus (itching) may also occur. The face is affected by allergies in rare cases. The chest and chest are usually affected - areas that have not been protected by radiation and were exposed directly to the sun.

Treatment

You can prevent sun allergy using an effective sunscreen cream specifically formulated to combat polymorphic dermatitis to light. Avoid strong mid-day radiation and always cover up with matching clothes. It is best to choose UV-protected clothes with tested effect. They are made of a special fabric that blocks UV penetration. In addition, those who suffer from sun allergy can do it light therapy before the warm season starts. This desensitizes the skin in the sun.
